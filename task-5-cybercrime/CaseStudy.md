# Case Study: UPI Fraud

This is Riya, a first-year college student, who has decided to sell her outdated textbooks in an online marketplace. This person contacted Riya a short time after she posted the advertisement and claimed to want to buy the books. The buyer promised to transfer the money immediately and asked for her UPI ID. 

After a couple of minutes, Riya got a payment request on her UPI app. The buyer instructed her to ”accept the request” and enter her UPI pin to transfer funds. She trusted the buyer and followed the instructions by entering the PIN. She was defrauded of ₹5,000 instead of receiving money. 

The fraudster immediately blocked her and was no longer available. Riya was deceived into approving a payment instead of getting one. This was reported, but it proved hard to recover the money. 

Students and young people who use apps for digital payments a lot but who don’t know how payment requests work are common targets for this kind of fraud. The consequences are a loss of funds, stress, and a loss of confidence in online payments. 

This case highlights the importance of awareness about UPI systems and the need to verify every transaction carefully before approving it. 

# Stay Safe Online Checklist
Never share your UPI PIN with anyone
Do not approve unknown “collect requests” on UPI apps
Always verify the sender before making payments
Avoid clicking on suspicious links or messages
Use strong and unique passwords for all accounts
Enable two-factor authentication wherever possible
Regularly check bank statements for unusual activity
Do not share OTPs with anyone

## UPI Safety Tips:

Remember: You do NOT need to enter a PIN to receive money
Double-check payment requests before approving
